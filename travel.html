Travel

Welcome to the Travel section of Arcane’s Melody! Here, you'll find tips and advice on how to travel on a budget, especially if you are a student. I will share my personal experiences of traveling alone as a college student, including my trips to NYC. I used Southwest Airlines for my travel, and I'll provide insights into my experiences with them. Additionally, I'll discuss budget-friendly travel tips and how to make the most of your trips without breaking the bank.

Background

I have always wished to visit NYC, especially since I was 18. When I got my first “big girl job” through an internship at my college,
I wanted to treat myself as a belated happy 18th/19th birthday gift. I looked up prices on Google and checked what fit my schedule and budget.
Most prices were $200 or more, which was out of my budget. I came across Southwest Airlines, and their prices were reasonable for a college student
on a budget. The downside is that certain days are more expensive than others, and sometimes the flights don't match your schedule. However, overall,
their prices are better than other airlines on the market. As mentioned, I am only a college student and do not have much money to spend, so I had to improvise.

During the summer of 2024, I secured an internship at my college. My work week was 4 hours a day, 20 hours a week, with a pay rate of $17.00 an hour.
You can imagine that I was on a very tight budget and needed to be cost-effective with expenses such as airfare, hotel/hostel stay, food, and any other
spending. I started looking at times when the airline had cheaper flights and then at my own schedule. I also had to ensure that my paycheck would align
with my previous two concerns. I managed to get a flight that would leave on September 6, 2024, at around 6 am, departing from Chicago MDW airport to
NYC LaGuardia airport.

I will add my itinerary with pictures to show what I did and any screenshots I took of the flight costs and anything else I bought in NYC. Please
note that this is my only trip at the moment, but I will be traveling more in the future when I save up.

Choosing a Flight

Deciding to choose a flight was difficult as I was still working at my college for the internship and my Fall semester was almost about to begin.
Southwest Airlines allows you to look at their calendar with the dates and prices labeled on each date. I found that September 6th was the most convenient
time. Thankfully, I was taken to the airport and did not need to spend money traveling to Midway Airport. As it was an early flight, I had breakfast at the
airport: yogurt with fruit and a water bottle.
I only had a backpack, so no checked bag.

Landing and Traveling to the Hostel

Right after landing, I followed the instructions at the airport and found my way downstairs to the buses that would take me to the subway.
I was nervous as I thought I would need to pay to get on the bus, but I was pleasantly surprised to know that the ride was free. I followed my
instructions on Google Maps, got lost, but eventually found my way to the hostel and left my bag so that I would only carry a handbag.
