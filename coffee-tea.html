Coffee & Tea

Welcome to the Coffee & Tea section of Arcane’s Melody! Here, you’ll find reviews and recommendations based on my personal experiences and preferences. I focus on sharing my thoughts on my favorite Dunkin’ orders, other coffee drinks from cafes I’ve visited, coffee creamers I’ve tried, and tea brands I’ve bought. Additionally, I’ll share some brewing methods that I’ve experimented with and enjoyed.

Cold Brew Maker

I have a couple of coffee makers that I personally use, and some are more pleasant experiences to use and for the taste of the coffee and tea. Recently,
I bought a Starbucks cold brew maker, which is perfect for making smooth, rich cold brew coffee at home. I ended up losing the instructions, but I found a helpful video to guide me.
Overall, I like the cold brew maker because you simply add the coffee grounds in the middle, put the filter on top, and the cap, which also serves as the opener for pouring coffee.
After adding the coarse coffee grounds and water, it’s best to shake it to help the coffee mix with the water, then let it sit in the fridge for 12-24 hours to enjoy smooth and
refreshing cold brew. This method does take longer and can be tedious if you do not want to wait but it’s a good cup of Cold brew.

French Press

I also use a Frensch Press, a classic method of making coffee. You add coarse coffee grounds to the French press, add hot water, let it steep for about 4-5 minutes, then press
the plunger down slowly to ensure all the coffee grounds are taken to the bottom. This method is faster if you want to enjoy a warm cup of coffee, and if you can also make iced coffee
quickly without waiting as long as for a cold brew. I enjoy using my French press because it’s faster making a nice warm cup of coffee, especially in this cold water.

Pour Over Coffee Maker

The pour-over coffee maker is a favorite among coffee enthusiasts because it produces a clean flavor. You can either grind beans for the best results or buy pre-grounded coffee.
Boil water to about 200F, place your filter in the dripper, and rinse it with hot water to remove any taste and preheat the dripper. Add the coffee grounds (about 1-2 tablespoons
per 6 ounces of water) to the filter. Start by pouring a small amount of water over the ground to let them bloom for about 30 seconds. Then, slowly pour the rest of the water in a
circular motion, ensuring all the grounds are evenly saturated.

Moka Pot

Lastly, I use an espresso stovetop Coffee Maker (Moka Pot), an Italian method for making strong, espresso-like coffee at home. Fill the bottom chamber with water up to the safety valve.
After placing the filter, fill the basket with finely ground coffee, level it off without packing it, and screw the top chamber on tightly. Place the Moka pot on the stove over medium heat.
The water will boil and create pressure, pushing the coffee through the filter into the top chamber.

Tea

I personally used a little metal tea infuser as it is easy to use with your loose-leaf tea as that carries more flavor than tea bags. You simply fill the infuser with loose tea
leaves, place it in your cup, pour hot water over it, let it steep for about 5 minutes and enjoy.

Using any teapot, you will boil your water and then add your loose tea left into the teapot and then add your water, letting it steep for 5 minutes and then using a
tea strainer to place it on top of the teacup you will pour the tea.

Lastly, I had a Teapot that was from Ikea. Itcame with an infusere and that also saved much time from the previous two methods as it was convenient to have two things in one.
