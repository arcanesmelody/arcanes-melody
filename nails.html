Nails

Welcome to the Nails section of Arcane’s Melody! Here, you will find recommendations for press-on nails, taking into
consideration my nail beds. I’ll share the products I use, where I find them, my thoughts on what I like, and tips on how to take care of your nails.

To begin, I will show a picture of my nails with only Essie Hard to Resist applied. I will then explain my process
for applying a French set on my nails, including the dos and don’ts. I will also share pictures of different marks
on my nails taken on different days while wearing the French set, followed by a picture of my nails right after removing
the set. Finally, I will take a picture of my nails as they are right now. But first I will share how i apply my nails and
then move on to what was mentioned above.

Applying Nails

Materials:

  • Nail set: I used Kiss Salon Acrylic French in the size Medium
  • Essie Hard to Resist nail strengthener
  • Nail clipper
  • Cuticle pusher
  • Kiss nail glue Mega Hold (any Kiss nail glue is good)
  • Nail filer
  • Acetone or alcohol wipes

Steps:

  1. Trim Your Nails: Start by cutting your nails if they are long. Even if they are short,
    it is better to have them trimmed to a manageable length.
  2. Prepare the Press-Ons: Take the nails out of the packaging and ensure that the nails you
    choose cover your entire nail bed without squishing your nails. Set them aside.
  3. Push Back Cuticles: Start pushing back your cuticles with a cuticle pusher and make sure
    to remove the dead cuticle.
  4. Cut the Cuticle: When cutting the cuticle, it should not hurt as you are only cutting the
    dead skin with the nail clipper. This helps your press-ons last longer.
  5. File the Nails: File the top of your nails to allow the glue and the press-on nail to
    grip your natural nails better.
  6. Clean the Nail Beds: Clean all your nail beds with acetone or alcohol wipes to ensure
    everything is clean.
  7. Optional Step: Apply a coat of Essie Hard to Resist to protect your nails. My nails
    last longer even with the Essie coat on.
  8. Apply the Glue: When you first start applying your nails, it may be difficult to
    handle the glue as it dries quickly. Apply a thin layer on your natural nail and another on your press-on nail.
  9. Attach the Press-Ons: Make sure you are applying your nail at a 45-degree angle and that each
    corner is touching the other corner of your nail. Hold the press-on nail for about 30 seconds to ensure there are no air bubbles.

Press-On Nails: Application, Maintenance, and Removal

I usually buy my press-on nails from Family Dollar because they are cheaper. For example, the French set I was wearing cost $3 at Family
Dollar, but it was $8 at Walgreens. I recommend Walgreens nails as they have some with different rhinestones, depending on what they have in
stock and the Walgreens near you. Both Family Dollar and Walgreens sell Kiss nails, which I prefer to use. Walgreens has some more intricate
designs, but you might find the same ones from Walgreens at Family Dollar for a lower price. With my instructions, my nails usually last 10 days.
After 9-10 days, the area around the cuticle starts to look unflattering, and they feel like they might pop off. This depends on a person's nail growth,
as I have heard from people that press-on's can last a month or more, but the packaging recommends only wearing them for a week or seven days. I also
think you should consider that leaving press-ons on for a long time can still damage your nails, so please keep that in mind.

As you can see, when I applied my nails, there were some air bubbles. This is not good as it can allow water to stay between your nails
and the press-ons, leading to bacteria growth.
You can also see that after some time, my nails lose their sharpness around the edges and become more rounded. You can definitely file them
to keep them sharper. I personally do not mind, but this is also why I only tend to extend to 10 days.

This time, I lasted around 11 days with my nails on as I wanted to see how long they would last. While in the grocery store, I was carrying
a pack of water bottles and lost a nail as I was putting it in the shopping cart. After that, I was able to pop off my nails by picking at one
side of the nail to remove it. I do not recommend this as it damages your nails. The only reason I feel it was easy to remove them like this is
because I had the Essie coat underneath, and it was past the 10 days to remove my nails.

Removing Nails

I have seen many people use dish soap in warm water, adding oil, and then placing their hands in the mixture to allow the nails to be removed
gently. They use a cuticle pusher to remove the nails.

Other people have also chosen to use acetone on their nails with cotton to allow for easier removal.
